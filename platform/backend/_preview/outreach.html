I'm currently studying Clinical Informatics at the University of Washington and have been building a prototype exploring patient-mediated coordination of care workflows.

The system models a patient-controlled interface where individuals can approve record access, review clinical notes, and help route fulfillment tasks (labs, prescriptions, referrals) across providers, pharmacies, and insurers.

The goal is to explore whether giving patients visibility and limited control over these workflows could reduce some of the coordination friction that exists between organizations.

How does this work with existing EHR systems?

The system connects through SMART on FHIR APIs and functions as a coordination layer — it does not replace the EHR.

✓ Reads from EHR systems via standardized FHIR R4 endpoints
✓ Does not write back or modify clinical data
✓ Works alongside Epic, Cerner & MEDITECH — not instead of them
✓ Patient-scoped OAuth 2.0 — patients authorize each connection
